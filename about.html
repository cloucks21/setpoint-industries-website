About

Twenty years in the channels we serve. Now buying the companies that serve them.

Setpoint Industries is being built as the next great service company in commercial refrigeration. The operator running it has spent two decades inside the grocery, convenience, and distribution channels that depend on this work. The capital partner behind it is built for the long hold. Together that is the business.

A point of view

Refrigeration is a service business.

The most profitable refrigeration companies are not the ones with the slickest CRMs. They are the ones whose techs pick up on the second ring. The ones whose store managers know the dispatcher by first name. The ones who have held a regional grocery account for twenty-five years because they showed up early, every time. The work is the relationship. The relationship is the work.

The temptation in this industry is to financialize that. Optimize the route, squeeze the margin, automate the dispatch, exit in three years. We have no interest in that approach. The companies worth owning are the ones that have built something durable. The job of a good acquirer is to keep what works working.

How we engage

What a conversation with Setpoint looks like.

Five stages between a first email and a closed transaction. None of them are surprises.

Stage 01

Introduction call

A no-pitch first conversation. We want to understand the business, the customer book, the team, and what you might want next. If there is no fit, we will say so on this call.

60 minutes

Stage 02

Site visit

We come to you. Walk the office, ride along on a service call if you would like, meet whoever you want us to meet. Most decisions about fit are made in this visit, not on a spreadsheet.

One to two days, on your schedule

Stage 03

Letter of intent

A written offer at a real number, not a range. Structure, price, closing conditions, and the transition plan. No longer than two pages. Yours to take to whatever advisor you want involved.

Usually two to three weeks after the site visit

Stage 04

Diligence

Quality of earnings, customer concentration review, equipment and fleet assessment, legal. Coordinated to be minimally invasive on your day-to-day. We work to your team's schedule, not the other way around.

Forty-five to ninety days

Stage 05

Close

The day the company changes hands. A binder, a wire, a handshake, and the start of whatever transition you have designed with us. Some owners step out at close. Others stay on for years. Both are fine.

A single day

How we are structured

Built for the long hold.

Setpoint operates with committed capital, not a fund that has to deploy by a certain date. The capital partner behind us has spent more than a decade backing operators through ownership transitions across multiple service industries. They built the structure. We choose the businesses and run them.

What that means in practice. The pace of diligence matches the pace of your decision. Transition design is yours to shape. And the same people you meet on the first call are the people who will be at the table on closing day.
