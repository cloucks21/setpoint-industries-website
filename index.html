How to read us

Three things we will not do.

Consolidate or relocate your team.

Service techs, dispatchers, route supervisors, account managers. Your roster is the company. Touching it would defeat the reason we showed up. We have no plans to centralize dispatch, regionalize routes, or rationalize headcount.

Run a fund clock or a flip plan.

We are not building Setpoint to exit in three years. Our capital partner is structured for the long hold. So is our plan. The pace of diligence and the pace of any change after close will match what the business actually needs.

Quote a number on the first call.

Valuation is a conversation. We need to understand the work, the team, and what you might want next before we can talk price honestly. Any acquirer who throws out a multiple in the first hour is selling, not buying.

Where we focus

The companies we are looking for.

Service-led commercial refrigeration businesses with a durable book of recurring customers. We look nationally. Adjacent service lines are considered when the customer overlap is real.

Commercial Refrigeration Service

Service, repair, and preventive maintenance for grocery, convenience, food service, and cold storage customers. The recurring-revenue heart of the business. A route, a tech, a phone that gets answered, and a customer base that has been on contract for a decade.

Cold Storage & Distribution Support

Refrigeration, controls, and mechanical service for cold storage operators, food distributors, and the warehouses that move the cold chain. Adjacent work to the grocery channel, same operating standards, often the same customers.

Adjacent Commercial Service Lines

Commercial HVAC, mechanical, electrical, and controls work that shares a customer base with the refrigeration trade. The same grocers, c-stores, and distributors are on the bill. When the geography and the book align, we will look closely.

For founders considering a transition

Questions owners ask us.

The four questions that come up on almost every first call. We have answered them the same way every time, which is why we are answering them here.

What happens to my team after a transaction?

Service techs, dispatchers, route supervisors, account managers. The people doing the work are the work. We are not consolidating headcount or relocating positions. The team that built the company is the team that runs it.

How fast does this move?

At the pace of your decision. Some transactions close in six months. Others take eighteen. We are not on a fund clock and we do not have a deployment quota. The right deal at the right pace beats the wrong deal at any speed.

Are you bringing in your own management?

We are bringing in capital, an operating partner who came up in this industry, and an advisory team. Day-to-day leadership is whatever you have built. If you stay as CEO for three years, that is one transition. If you want to hand the keys over in six months, that is another. The model is yours.

Will the brand and the customer relationships change?

Not because we want them to. Your name, your reputation in the market, and the trust your customers have in your team are the reasons we are here. We will only revisit any of it if you ask us to.
